The Local Connection Gap

Despite living in dense cities, professionals and small businesses can't connect locally.

Problem #1: Professionals Can't Find Each Other Locally

The Gap: LinkedIn excels at global networking but lacks local proximity search.

Reality:

  • 73% of freelancers say "finding local clients" is #1 challenge
  • LinkedIn's algorithm prioritizes global connections over proximity
  • No integrated search: "accountant within 10km"
  • No built-in multimedia communication (chat + voice + video in one place)

Result: Professionals isolated despite living in same neighborhood. Work goes to platforms like TaskRabbit/Upwork (15-20% commission). Local economies weakened.

Problem #2: Small Businesses Lack Direct Customer Connection

The Gap: Google Maps/Yelp excel at discovery but lack owner communication.

Reality:

  • Google Ads: $500-$2,000/month to stay visible
  • Yelp: $300-$1,500/month for promoted listings
  • 60% of small businesses closed during pandemic
  • No direct messaging channel (customers can't reach owners)
  • No way to showcase "the person behind the business"

Result: Small businesses spend more on ads than revenue justifies. Discovery is pay-to-play, not merit or proximity-based.
