   - Identify who can introduce you

   - Search "[Your University] alumni at [VC Firm]"
   - Reach out via alumni association

   - Find founders of companies in their portfolio

## âš ï¸ COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID

1. **Spray and pray:** Don't send 100 generic emails. Better to send 30 personalized emails.

2. **Giving up too early:** 80% of deals happen at follow-up #3-5. Don't stop at one email.

3. **Not tracking outreach:** Use spreadsheet or Supabase to track every contact. You'll forget otherwise.

4. **Being defensive:** When VCs ask hard questions, don't get defensive. It's their job to poke holes.

5. **Taking too long:** Fundraising loses momentum. Strike while iron is hot. Move fast.

6. **Not creating urgency:** If you have interested investors, tell others. FOMO works on VCs.

7. **Bad follow-up:** Send thank you email within 2 hours of every meeting. Always.

8. **Ignoring rejections:** If someone passes, ask why. Feedback is valuable.

9. **Burning bridges:** Today's "no" might be tomorrow's "yes" at Series A. Stay gracious.

10. **Not asking for intros:** If a VC passes, ask "Who else should I talk to?" They often give referrals.
